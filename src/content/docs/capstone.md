### What this is

This is where the whole week becomes one piece of work. You'll design a small, complete marketing campaign — and because you'll pick the product yourself, you walk away with a real portfolio piece that's entirely yours.

### The brief

**Pick any product you like** — a real one you admire, or your own idea. Then design a small campaign for it that pulls together everything you've learned:

1. **The audience** — a specific ICP, not "everyone." *(Module 4)*
2. **The message** — the one core idea you want to land. *(Modules 4 & 6)*
3. **The copy** — write the actual words: a post, a headline + subhead, or a short piece of copy. Make it native, specific, and slop-free. *(Modules 5 & 6)*
4. **The channel** — pick one (X, LinkedIn, or another you can justify) and say why it fits this audience and product. *(Modules 4 & 5)*
5. **The metric** — name the one number that would prove the campaign worked, and say why it's the right one (not a vanity metric). *(Module 3)*
6. **Honesty check** — if your product involves AI or any bold claim, make sure you've described it truthfully. *(Modules 1 & 2)*

### The deliverable

A **one-page brief** containing all six pieces above, **plus the actual copy/post** written out, ready to publish.

### Rubric

| Criterion | What we're looking for |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **Integration** | It visibly pulls the modules together — audience, channel, message, copy, and metric all connect. |
| **Realism** | It's something a small team could actually run, not a fantasy with an infinite budget. |
| **Ship quality** | The copy is good enough to *use*, not just good enough to pass. Specific, native, no slop. |

Rated **Developing / Solid / Strong.**

### A final note

If you've done all six assignments and this capstone, you are no longer someone "new to marketing." You can explain a product, reason about numbers, plan a go-to-market, behave like a native on real platforms, and write copy a human would actually want to read. That's a real foundation — keep building on it.
