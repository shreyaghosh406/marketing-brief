**A one-week course for people who are new to marketing.**

This course turns curiosity into capability. In a week, you'll go from "I'm not sure what marketers actually do" to being able to explain a product clearly, read metrics without flinching, think in terms of go-to-market, behave like a native on real platforms, and write copy that doesn't sound like a robot wrote it.

A few things to know before you start:

- **You learn by doing.** Every module ends in a real assignment. Reading the lessons makes you informed; doing the assignments makes you capable. The assignments are the point — don't skip them.
- **Most of what you make, you keep.** Several assignments produce real work — a post, a plan, a piece of copy — that you can put in a portfolio. The final capstone is a campaign for a product *you* choose. That's yours.
- **No background needed.** If a term is new, it gets explained. There's no jargon here that we don't unpack first.

### The week

| Day | You'll learn | You'll make |
|-----|--------------|-------------|
| 1 | The product + how AI tools actually work | A plain-English product explanation; an AI explainer |
| 2 | How to read metrics | A diagnosis of a real-looking dashboard |
| 3 | Go-to-market thinking | A mini go-to-market plan |
| 4 | How to grow on real platforms | One genuinely postable contribution |
| 5 | Copywriting + putting it all together | Copy variations; a full mini-campaign |

Plan for roughly 1.5–3 hours a day. Day 1 and Day 5 are the heaviest.

### How each lesson is built

Every module follows the same shape so you always know where you are: **why it matters → the core ideas → a worked example → common mistakes → your assignment → optional further reading.** Each assignment has a rubric so you know exactly what "good" looks like before you start.
